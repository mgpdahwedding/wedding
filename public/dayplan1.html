ACTIVE DAY


RUN & SWIM START


Margaret Island (Margit-sziget) has a 5km running track around it which has beautiful views of the city. If you're up for it, or would rather it, swim some laps at Alfred Hajos Swimming Pool. And for a coffee on the way back to your accom, drop in to Waycup Coffee. Click the icon for the area.


COFFEE & BRUNCH


After you've showered up at your accommodation, try out one of these spots for coffee and something to eat. Dorado - our favourite breakfast and coffee spot. Portobello - great breakfast, brunch coffee and delicious sweet treats. SOLID - nice brunch amongst the rooftops of Budapest.


WALK


This walk in total (multiple sections below) will take approx 3 - 5 hours, depending on how often you stop, and will show you a lot of the key sites and best views in Budapest. First, walk west across Liberty Bridge towards the Buda side of the Danube. Climb Gellert Hill to Liberty Statue (Szabads√°g-szobor) in the citadella, taking in the 360 view. Climb down the other side and follow the river to Varket Bazar. Walk through the beautiful gardens and make your way up to Buda Castle. Take in the view at Buda Castle and perhaps visit the National Gallery inside. Follow the roads through the medieval old town of Budapest. Pit stop: You will pass by Ruzworm a pastry shop that has been around since 1827 and is famous for their custard cake. 4 Minute Cafe is also a good coffee spot too. Click the Google Maps icon for this route.


CONTINUE TO WALK


Continue to walk through the old streets to the Matthias Church with its unique roofing. Walk around and take in the views of the fairytale-like Fisherman's Bastion. If you are feeling cultural, pop your head in to Koller Gallery, an iconic modern art dealership founded in 1953. Start to make your way back down the hill towards the water. A traditional Hungarian lunch is the kind of lunch you need to have walked two hours for. Roma Etelbar, a small traditional family-run Hungarian restaurant which has recently, is close by. It's a must-try if you are interested in trying true Hungarian food in a traditional, friendly setting. Click the Google Maps icon to see this part of the route.


MORE WALKING


After a real Hungarian Lunch you will be happy to walk again. Walk along the water where you will have a magnificent view of Parliament House. You can make a pit stop to walk past Kiraly Thermals Baths - unfortunatelty closed but dates back to the 16th Century and is the oldest bath house in Budapest. Coffee Stop: Bambi - This is more of an experience than a 'go to' spot. A communist era cafe which has not changed at all! A visit back in time. Click the Google Maps icon to see this route.


EVEN MORE WALKING


Walk to Margaret Bridge and cross the Danube. You will walk past Margaret Island - we recommend resting in the beautiful gardens. On the other side of Margaret Bridge you will find one of the best Retes stores in Budapest, Hazi Retesbolt (cash only). You may not have room for more food, but you can always get take away. Cross back over the street and make your way down Falk Miksa Utca, Budapest's antique row. It's a street lined with antique stores and galleries. Follow Falk Miksa and you will come out at the Hungarian Parliament building, an imposing Gothic Revivial style building which is amazing. Continue down to Liberty Square for a lively park full of locals. In the summer there are often markets, concerts and bars popping up in this space. From Liberty Square you will walk throught the lively 5th district, which will lead you to your final stop, St Stephens Basilica, a Roman Catholic church which started construction in 1851. Recommended coffee stops along the way are Lui Coffee and Espresso Embassy. Click the Google Maps icon to see this route.


A WINE TO TAKE IT ALL IN


After all that walking, you will need to rest your legs and have a glass of wine or three. A short walk away is one of our favourite bars in Budapest, Marlou, owned by a lively french man JJ who is extremely knowledgable and very kind. A happy bonus, Marlou looks over the Opera House, ticking another site off the list. The Opera House has been recently renovated and has frequest performances. Click the Google Maps icon for this final part of the route.
