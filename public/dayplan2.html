CULTURAL DAY


BREAKFAST


Start the day with coffee and breakfast at Dorado. One of our favorite breakfast places in Budapeast.


WALK


A 15 minute walk from Dorado will take you to Saxlehner palota, a museum at the start of Andrassay Avenue. Referred to as "Budapest's Champs-Élysées," Andrássy Avenue is a UNESCO World Heritage boulevard that connects the city center with Heroes' Square and the City Park. Andrássy is lined with landmark buildings, and Renaissance Revival palazzos. Saxlehner palota, built in 1884 is one of the remaining palazzos.

Continue up Andrássy and you will pass Herend Porecelain, Hungary's answer to Royal Albert or Royal Copenhagen. Founded in 1825, Herend Poreclain is still made in Hungary and all hand painted. Incredibly expensive but worth a look.

Continue walking and you will pass the Hungarian State Opera House which has just undergone a full renovation, you can book a tour to go inside the building. Across from the opera house stands the less conspicuous but comparably impressive Drechsler Palace, another remaining noble palace (now the W hotel).

Continue on until you get to Oktogon (a large intersection) where we recommend a small detour to New York Cafe on Erzsebet Kortut. Whilst we don't recommend eating here, we do recommend stopping for an overpriced drink and taking in the building. Commissioned in 1884 by New York Life Insurance as their Hungarian headquarters, it has been a coffee house since 1930. One of the, if not the most, spectacular coffee houses in Europe. A baroque masterpiece.

WALK MORE


Make your way back to Andrassay and continue towards City Park. Andrassay has many amazing attractions including Robert Capa Photography Centre, Terror Museum (documenting the communist era), Liszt Museum and the University of Fine Arts.

A recommended detour just off Andrassay is Hunyadi Market, one of the five grand indoor markets that opened in Budapest at the end of the 19th century. The market's lángos shop is great, and there's a specialty coffee shop across the park named Cube.

Continue on Andrassay and take in the marvel of Kodály körönd, a beautiful, spacious roundabout lined with a park and impressive palazzos. In this section you will also find Ráth György-villa, Art Nouveau style ceramics, jewelry and furniture, displayed in a grand 19th-century villa.

A BIT MORE WALKING


The final section of Andrássy, between Kodály Körönd to Heroes' Square, is dotted with villas. The wealthy families who once lived here are long gone, having been persecuted by both the local Nazi and the subsequent Communist regimes. After the nationalizations, many buildings became embassies. Epreskert is a sculpture garden close by that’s worth visiting.

Andrassay terminates at Heroes' Square, Hungary's national monument built around the turn of the 20th century. Heroes Square is flanked by two museums The Museum of Fine Arts, the most important art museum in Hungary, and the Kunsthalle (Műcsarnok) which houses temporary exhibitions by local artists.

From Heroes' Square you can enter City Park, a large park with a lake, the Szechenyi Thermal Baths, Vajdahunyad Castle and the Budapest Zoo. The park is a great place to relax and take in the sights.
